Knafeh

This kanafa, or knafeh, recipe is a favorite Palestinian dessert. Crunchy shredded phyllo dough is baked with a layer of creamy sweet cheese and then drenched in rosewater syrup. It's simple yet impressive.

Ingredients

Knafeh:

  • Phyllo Dough [453 grams]
  • Ricotta Cheese [425 grams]e
  • 1 cup of shredded Mozzarella Cheese
  • 1/3 cup of White Sugar
  • Unsalted Butter [340 grams]

Syrup:

  • 1 cup of White Sugar
  • 1/2 cup of Water
  • 1 teaspoon of Lemon Juice
  • 1/8 teaspoon of Rose Water optional

Directions:

  1. Making the knafeh: Preheat the oven to 200 degrees celcius
  2. Shred the phyllo dough until the strands are about the size of a grain of rice. Pour into a large mixing bowl.
  3. Mix ricotta, mozzarella, and sugar in a separate bowl.
  4. Melt butter in a microwave until it melts, and let it sit until a white foam has formed on top.
  5. Pour the butter into the bowl of the phyllo dough, use your hands to mix the butter and the dough together. Make sure the butter is absorbed by the dough.
  6. Spread the buttered dough in a pan. Spread the cheese mixture onto the dough.
  7. Bake in the oven until the cheese is slightly golden and the edges of the dough are brown and bubbly 30-35 minutes
  8. Making the syrup: combine 1 cup sugar and water in a small saucepan. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium-high heat. Reduce the heat to medium and stir in lemon juice. Simmer, stirring constantly, until the sugar is dissolved and the mixture is thickened, 5 to 7 minutes. Be careful not to let the mixture turn golden and caramelize. Remove from heat and add rose water; set aside.
  9. Remove the knafeh from the oven. Place a platter or a baking sheet over the dish. Carefully place the dish onto the platter so that the phyllo is on top. Pour syrup over the knafeh. Cut into pieces and serve while hot.
